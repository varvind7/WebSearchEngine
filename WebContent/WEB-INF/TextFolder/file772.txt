Research In Motion has seen its sales fall behind the competition from Apple's iPhone and devices running Google's Android software. The Canadian company made a net loss for the three months to 3 March 2012 of $125m (£78m), compared with a profit of $934m a year earlier. Speaking to Wake Up To Money on BBC Radio 5 live Larry Magid from CNET said that the company may look at selling the consumer arm of the business.
