Fleye keeps its moving parts inside a cage covered with a protective grid, which the firm says means it can bump into people without the risk of injury. It has led the firm to claim that it is on verge of creating the "world's safest drone" - an important consideration at a time when incidents involving unmanned aircraft are on the rise.
