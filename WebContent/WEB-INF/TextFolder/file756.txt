The parent company Canonical is one of many hoping to replace phones, tablets, laptops and computers with just one device. But there is not yet a phone/PC hybrid running Ubuntu with the necessary monitor port available for consumers to buy. The demonstration at Mobile World Congress featured the Linux-based OS loaded onto an old handset. BBC's Chris Foxx asked Ubuntu's founder Mark Shuttleworth whether the firm would be playing catch-up with HP which announced a hybrid handset running Windows 10 at the Barcelona tech show.
